# Character Registry: SEOUL IDENTITY

## Main Characters

### JAKE COOPER

#### Basic Info
- **Full Name:** Jake Cooper
- **Script Name:** JAKE
- **First Appearance:** Scene 1, Page 1
- **Age:** 23
- **Role:** Protagonist

#### Physical Description
- **Hair:** Brown, kept neat (teacher persona) but disheveled under stress
- **Build:** Athletic but not imposing - runner's build, quick rather than strong
- **Distinguishing Features:** American features that stand out in Seoul; observant eyes that betray training

#### Character Core
- **Want (External):** Complete his mission, prove himself to the CIA
- **Need (Internal):** Discover who he truly is beyond his manufactured identities
- **Lie Believed:** "I know exactly who I am and who I work for"
- **Ghost/Wound:** Recruited young, never had chance to form authentic identity
- **Truth:** Identity is a choice, not an assignment

#### Voice Profile
| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Education** | College-educated, articulate but not pretentious |
| **Vocabulary** | Precise when calm, American idioms slip out under stress |
| **Korean ability** | Fluent with flawless accent - his greatest asset |
| **Rhythm** | Short sentences when operational, longer when relaxed |
| **Tells** | Fingers tap when anxious, goes quiet when lying |
| **Avoids** | Personal questions, past tense, the word "home" |

#### Speech Patterns
**When teaching (cover persona):**
- Warm, encouraging, patient
- "Good job. Now try it again with feeling."
- Uses Korean naturally, switches to English for emphasis

**When operational (spy mode):**
- Clipped, efficient, no wasted words
- "Where's the package?" / "We're blown. Move."
- Reverts to English under pressure

**When vulnerable (rare):**
- Hesitant, unfinished thoughts
- "I thought I knew... but now I'm not sure I ever..."
- Voice drops, eye contact breaks

#### Sample Lines
```
CONTROLLED:
"The handoff is at midnight. Don't be late."

TEACHING:
"No, no - say it like you're asking a question. 'Where is the station?' Try again."

UNDER PRESSURE:
"Dammit. They knew. They knew the whole time."

REVELATION:
"I've been running their playbook from day one. Every choice I thought was mine..."
```

#### Knowledge State
| By Scene | Knows | Doesn't Know |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| Opening | His mission, his contacts | He's been manipulated |
| Midpoint | Something doesn't add up | The full scope of betrayal |
| All Is Lost | He's been a pawn for both sides | What to do next |

#### Arc Summary
**Start:** Confident operative with clear identity
**Middle:** Cracks appear as reality doesn't match intel
**Low Point:** Everything he believed was a lie
**End:** Forges authentic identity through choice, not assignment

- **Script Name:** MR. HAN
- **First Appearance:** Scene 6

- **Role:** Catalyst/Mentor figure (ambiguous loyalty)

### SUNG-HO

#### Basic Info
- **Full Name:** Sung-ho (family name unknown)
- **Script Name:** SUNG-HO
- **First Appearance:** Scene 14
- **Age:** 40s
- **Role:** Ally/B-Story relationship

#### Physical Description
- **Build:** Working class, strong hands from labor
- **Demeanor:** Weathered but warm
- **Expression:** Worry lines, but genuine smile

#### Relationship to Jake
- Old connection (nature to be revealed)
- Provides safe harbor in chaos
- Represents possible authentic human connection
- Contrast to Jake's isolated spy existence

#### Voice Profile
- Korean primarily, limited English
- Speaks from the gut, not the head
- Proverbs and folk wisdom
- Emotional transparency Jake lacks

#### Sample Lines
```
"You look like hell. When did you last sleep?"

"I don't need to know why. I just need to know you're not getting me killed."

"America, Korea - these are just lines on maps. What matters is who stands with you when the lines don't matter anymore."
```
